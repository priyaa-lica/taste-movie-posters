# Modern Minimalist Movie Poster — Visual Skill

## When to Use
This aesthetic is suitable for creating contemporary movie posters that seek to stand out with simplicity and direct messaging. It applies to dramas, indie films, and stylized commercial features where visual coherence and impact are prioritized.

## Aesthetic Identity
The aesthetic is clean, bold, and intentional. It highlights restrained compositions, contrasting color schemes, and minimal use of elements for visual clarity. Typography and imagery are the key focal points, with a lean towards modernist sensibilities.

## Color System
The palette typically uses bold primary colors like red, white, and black with restrained accent shades. Saturation is either high for boldness or completely muted for minimalism. Gradients and unnecessary color variations are avoided. Neon and pastel colors are excluded. 

## Composition Rules
Content is centered or strategically aligned, balancing negative space with dense focal elements. Strong use of symmetry or intentional asymmetry dominates. Imagery and text are distinct, with clear visual hierarchy to emphasize key messages. Layouts avoid clutter and peripheral distractions.

## Typography
Sans-serif typefaces are preferred, with clean lines and no decorative elements. A mix of bold fonts and regular weights creates hierarchy, with selectively stylized type treatments (e.g., tight kerning or contrasting case). Fonts remain unadorned, without serifs or unnecessary embellishments.

## Material & Surface
Digital polish with smooth, flat colors and precise edges. Surfaces evoke clarity, avoiding texture or materiality. Visuals lean towards illustrations, silhouettes, or clean-cut photographic elements with high contrast.

## Cultural Anchors
Influences stem from modernist graphic design, indie film promotional materials, and contemporary art posters. The aesthetic reflects trends from the 2010s and 2020s while rejecting overused CGI-heavy and montage styles typical of traditional Hollywood promotion.

## Exclusions
- Gradients and multicolor schemes.
- Decorative or ornate fonts.
- Textured or grungy designs.
- Overly complex or montage-like visuals.
- Excessive secondary text or visual clutter.

## Generative Instructions
1. Start with a bold, high-contrast color scheme (e.g., red/white/black).
2. Center or align visual and text elements with clear hierarchy.
3. Use sans-serif fonts in bold and/or regular weights based on importance.
4. Keep the overall design minimal, with prominent whitespace or clear divisions.
5. Focus the composition on one main visual: a stylized silhouette, key figure, or iconic object.
6. Avoid gradients and ensure colors remain flat and bold.
7. Maintain sharp, clean edges on all elements.
8. Incorporate a single accent color sparingly if contrast is needed.
9. Avoid textual details beyond the movie title, tagline, and limited credits.
10. Present the poster with precise alignment and professional polish.
